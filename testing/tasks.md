
# Critical User Tasks — Validation

**Source brief:** HiveMind × Ravensbourne brief, Section 5
**Source handbook:** WBL student handbook, Week 2

These are the three task scenarios the high-fidelity prototype was built around and tested against.

---

## Task A — Find a specific document in under 3 clicks

**Scenario given to participant:** *"It's your second week. You need to find your company's expense policy. Find the most recent version of that document."*

- **Expected path:** Dashboard → Resources → SOPs → [document]
- **Click target:** ≤ 3 clicks
- **Pain point being tested:** Scattered information / no single source of truth
- **Hypothesis tested:** H1, H2 (`/docs/hypotheses.md`)

---

## Task B — See what to work on today

**Scenario given to participant:** *"You're starting your day. In 30 seconds, tell me what you're working on, what's blocked, and what's coming up this week."*

- **Expected path:** Dashboard (everything visible on home) → Tasks (drill-down if needed)
- **Click target:** 0–1 clicks (most should be visible from the home screen)
- **Pain point being tested:** No clear daily priority
- **Hypothesis tested:** Implied by H1 (single source of truth)

---

## Task C — Know who to ask, and understand the AI assistant

**Scenario given to participant:** *"You have a question about pricing. Find the right person to ask. Then ask the AI assistant the same question and tell me whether you trust the answer it gives you."*

- **Expected path (Part 1):** Directory → Go-to-market team → [member]
- **Expected path (Part 2):** Resources → AI search → [reply with "cites sources"]
- **Click target:** ≤ 3 clicks per part
- **Pain point being tested:** No view of teammates + lack of trust in AI tools
- **Hypothesis tested:** H3, H4

This is the most important of the three tasks because it tests two pain points and stresses the AI transparency design specifically.

---

## How tasks were observed

For each task and each participant we recorded:
- Success / partial success / fail
- Click count
- Hesitation moments (where they paused or visibly read the screen)
- Confidence rating at the end (1–5)
- Verbatim quote during the think-aloud

Notes were stored in `/testing/session-notes/`.
