
# Test Script — Validation Sessions

**Lead:** Md Hossain (UX Research and Validation Lead)
**Sprint week:** Week 4
**Format:** 25–30 minute video calls, think-aloud method
**Tool used in session:** Figma prototype (https://www.figma.com/design/AihgLBor13wrL6hCALjg16/Hivemind-Final-Dashboard)

---

## 1. Intro & consent (2 minutes)

> "Thanks for taking the time. I'm a final-year Computer Science student at Ravensbourne, working on a real product brief for a company called HiveMind. They're building an onboarding tool for new hires. I'm going to show you a clickable prototype and ask you to do a few small tasks while talking out loud about what you see and what you'd do.
>
> A few things up front: this is testing the design, not testing you. There are no wrong answers. If something is confusing, that's our problem, not yours. I'd love it if you could be as honest as possible — including the negative.
>
> The session will take about 25 minutes. Are you happy for me to take written notes? Nothing will be published with your name on it — only the role or sector you work in. You can stop at any time."

✅ Wait for verbal "yes" before continuing.

---

## 2. Warm-up (3 minutes)

> "Before we look at the prototype — quick context. In your own job, what does your first hour of the day usually look like? And when you joined, how did you find your feet in the first week or two?"

(Two open-ended questions to set the participant up for the think-aloud and capture a baseline before they see HiveMind.)

---

## 3. Task A — Finding a document (5 minutes)

> "I'm going to share my screen now. This is the prototype. Imagine it's your second week at this company. You need to find your company's expense policy and you need to make sure it's the most recent version. Take me through what you'd do."

**Observe:**
- Click count
- Whether they notice the version tags ("V2.4") and "Verified" badge
- Whether they hesitate between Dashboard previews and the full Resources page

**Probe (only if they get stuck):** "What would you click next?"

---

## 4. Task B — Daily priority (5 minutes)

> "OK, new day. You've just opened the product. In 30 seconds, tell me what you're working on, what's blocked, and what's coming up this week."

**Observe:**
- Whether the home screen alone is enough, or whether they go to Tasks
- Whether they correctly identify the blocked item
- Whether the daily standup callout is noticed

---

## 5. Task C — Who to ask + AI trust (8 minutes)

**Part 1:**
> "You have a pricing question. Find the right person you'd ask."

**Part 2:**
> "Now, instead of asking that person, ask the AI assistant the same question. Read the reply out loud. On a scale of 1 to 5, how much would you trust that answer? Why?"

**Observe (this is the most important task):**
- Whether they reach the right team / person
- Whether they notice the "cites sources" label on the AI reply
- Whether they click through to the source
- Their verbatim trust-rating reason

---

## 6. Closing questions (4 minutes)

1. "On a scale of 1 to 5, how prepared would you feel for your first day at a new company if this was their tool?"
2. "In one sentence, what is this product for?"
3. "If you could change one thing about it, what would it be?"
4. "Was anything confusing or annoying that I haven't asked about?"

---

## 7. SUS questionnaire (3 minutes)

10-item System Usability Scale, scored 1 ("strongly disagree") to 5 ("strongly agree"). Standard SUS wording.

> **Note:** SUS was prepared and administered where time allowed. In some sessions the closing questions ran over and SUS was skipped. This is acknowledged as a limitation in `/delivery/validation-report.md`, Section 7.

---

## 8. Thank-you and close

> "Really helpful, thank you. If anything occurs to you in the next day or two, you've got my email. The validation report will be in our public GitHub repo if you'd like to see how your feedback gets used."
